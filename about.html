About Me

I am a trained professional organizer with a passion for problem solving. It gives me great joy to help people organize and simplify their homes and their lives so that they can enjoy their homes to the fullest, be more productive, experience less stress, and achieve peace of mind. I bring expertise, and a caring and professional style to my work.

I have 30 years’ experience in senior management positions and I have a proven track record of successfully developing and implementing complex projects

My education includes two university degrees (Bachelor of Environmental Studies, Bachelor of Arts), and I have earned my Project Management Professional (PMP) and Lean Six Sigma Green Belt (LSSGB) designations. I have also completed my Professional Organizers Training with the Professional Organizers of Canada.

When I’m not organizing something, I’m volunteering with Carpenter Hospice as a Board member, with Port Nelson United Church on the Refugee Sponsorship Committee, or with the Rotary Club of Burlington North where I am a past president (2013/2014) and have been twice awarded the Paul Harris Fellowship Award in recognition of my outstanding contributions to Rotary exemplifying the highest ideal in Rotary placing “Service Above Self”.

I am also a proud mother and a grandmother to two beautiful little boys.

As a member of the Professional Organizers in Canada, I maintain a strict code of ethics to ensure credibility and accountability while maintaining a high standard in the organizing industry.
